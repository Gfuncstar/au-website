You've reached the end of the Mini.

In two short lessons you've now seen:

- That acne is a **chronic inflammatory disorder of the pilosebaceous unit**, not a hygiene problem
- The **four mechanisms** that drive every breakout — sebum, hyperkeratinisation, *C. acnes* imbalance, inflammation
- Why most plans fail by treating one or two of those mechanisms instead of all four

That awareness alone changes consultations. But it doesn't yet give you a **defensible treatment plan**.

- The **NICE-aligned severity grading** (mild / moderate / severe) and what it changes

- The **treatment-pathway decision tree** — what you treat in clinic, what gets a referral, what's beyond your scope
- AHAs, BHAs, retinoids, oral pathways — when each belongs and how to sequence without burning the barrier
- **Real UK case studies** (mild comedonal, moderate inflammatory, severe / scarring risk, hormonal adult)
- The **red flags** that mean "refer to GP / dermatology" — non-negotiable
- A **Certificate of Completion** for CPD evidence

It's the difference between *understanding* acne and *treating* it.
