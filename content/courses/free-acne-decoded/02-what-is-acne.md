Most acne plans don't work because they treat **the lesion**, not **the condition**. To treat the condition, you have to know what it actually is.

## Definition

Acne (medically *acne vulgaris*) is a **chronic inflammatory disorder of the pilosebaceous unit**, the hair follicle and its associated sebaceous (oil) gland. It is not a hygiene problem. It is not a teenage phase. It is a long-term inflammatory condition that needs long-term management.

According to **NICE**, acne is a condition that ranges from mild to severe and **often requires long-term management rather than short-term treatment**. That single sentence reshapes how you should plan every consultation.

## The four mechanisms underneath every breakout

Every active acne lesion is the visible end of four interlocking processes happening underneath the skin. The full course unpacks each in clinical detail. The headline:

### 1. Excess sebum production

Androgens (testosterone, DHEA, DHT) stimulate the sebaceous glands. Excess sebum doesn't cause acne on its own, but it changes the follicular environment in a way that allows everything else downstream.

### 2. Follicular hyperkeratinisation

Dead keratinocytes inside the follicle stop shedding normally. They mix with sebum to form a plug, the **microcomedone**. This is the earliest stage of acne, and it happens *before* you can see anything on the surface.

If the follicle stays closed → whitehead. If it opens and oxidises → blackhead.

### 3. *Cutibacterium acnes* imbalance

*C. acnes* is a normal resident of healthy skin. In acne-prone follicles, the blocked low-oxygen environment lets it multiply. It breaks sebum down into inflammatory by-products. **Acne is not an infection**, it's an inflammatory response to a bacterial imbalance. That distinction matters when choosing topicals.

### 4. Inflammatory & immune response

The immune system reacts. Blood vessels dilate, white cells migrate in, and the visible lesions (papules, pustules, nodules, cysts) appear. Severe or prolonged inflammation increases the risk of **post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH)** and **permanent scarring**.

## Why this changes the plan

If you only treat one of those four mechanisms, you fail. Most over-the-counter regimens treat one or two. Most clinic plans built from social media treat one or two. **NICE-aligned plans treat all four**, sequenced correctly, with the barrier kept intact.

This is the difference between **managing acne** and **chasing acne**.
