## 1. Your case study

Pick a current or recent client whose presentation lands somewhere in the territory you've covered, multi-condition, complex, or stalled. Walk them through the framework you've now learned.

**Case study structure:**

- **Demographic + presentation**, age, hormonal stage, primary concern, secondary concerns
- **History (structured)**, onset, duration, pattern, triggers, previous treatments, medical history, family history
- **Examination findings**, distribution, lesion types, skin tone, barrier baseline, photographic baseline
- **Classification statement**, condition + subtype + severity + driver
- **Plan**, modalities, sequencing, timelines, risk hierarchy
- **Documentation**, consent, photographic baseline, capacity note
- **Outcome** (if available), response to treatment, learning points

**Length: 1–2 pages.** Not a write-up of every detail, a clinical reasoning narrative.

**This case study is yours to keep.** It's evidence of clinical reflection, useful as CPD documentation, and a reference point you can come back to as your practice matures.

## 2. Self-audit against NICE alignment

Run your own clinical practice through this audit. Honest answers; no judgement.

**Foundations:**

- Do I have a clear mental model of the four functional skin layers?
- Do I assess barrier function at every consultation?
- Do I differentiate skin types from skin conditions in my classification?

**Consultation:**

- Do I run a structured intake form?
- Do I ask history questions in the same order, every time?
- Do I document examination findings systematically?
- Do I classify with subtype + severity + driver?
- Do I set realistic expectations explicitly?
- Do I document a cooling-off period?
- Do I have version-dated, treatment-specific consent forms?

**Conditions:**

- Acne, am I addressing all four mechanisms in every plan?
- Rosacea, am I running barrier-first protocols?
- Pigmentation, is photoprotection framed as treatment?
- Barrier, am I identifying functional vs structural impairment?
- Photodamage, am I using Glogau classification?
- Scarring, am I documenting realistic 12-month timelines?

**Combination therapy:**

- Am I sequencing actives at one new per 4 weeks?
- Am I scheduling downtime around procedures realistically?
- Am I documenting risk in every consent conversation?

**Documentation:**

- Could I reconstruct any current client's plan from their file alone?
- Am I photographing in standardised lighting / angles?
- Am I logging adverse events even when minor?

The audit is a baseline. Score yourself honestly. The action items are the gaps.

## 3. Documentation file build-out

By the end of this programme, you should have:

- **Standardised consultation flow**, printed reference, kept at your desk
- **Intake form template**, digital, sent before the consult
- **Consent forms**, version-dated, treatment-specific, one per modality
- **Aftercare leaflets**, current versions, given to every client at every treatment
- **Photographic protocol**, same lighting, angles, expression, distance
- **Adverse event log**, even minor events recorded
- **CPD log**, rolling 12-month, with reflective notes
- **Scope-of-practice statement**, what you do, what you don't, what you refer

This is the **defensible practice file**. It's what makes every plan defensible, to a GP query, an insurer review, a complaint, a CQC enquiry, or a regulator inspection.

If any of these are missing, this is the action list for the next 30 days.

## 4. CPD reflection prompts

For your CPD portfolio (NMC revalidation, GMC appraisal, voluntary register, employer requirements):

**Reflection prompts:**

- Which condition module changed how I think about a current client?
- Where in my consultation flow do I see the biggest gap between current practice and the Skin Specialist framework?
- What's one specific change I'll make in clinic this week as a result of this programme?
- Which referral pathway am I most uncertain about, and what's my next learning step there?
- How does NICE-aligned thinking change my conversation with clients about realistic outcomes?

Write 200–500 words on at least three of these prompts. File in your CPD portfolio. **This is reflective practice evidence**, not paperwork.
