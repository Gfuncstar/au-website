## Why the consultation is the single highest-leverage moment

Everything you've learned in Modules 1 to 7 lives or dies at the consultation. A great topical sequence delivered by an unclear consultation is wasted. A clear consultation with a moderate protocol holds the client and outperforms a strong protocol with a confusing one.

This module is the operational layer: the consultation script, the timeline, the photo protocol, and the cadence.

## The seven-question consultation

Run these in order. Fifteen minutes total, not including face reading.

### Question 1. When did you first notice the pigment?

Anchor the timeline. Recent (under twelve months) often points to a specific trigger. Long-standing (years) means the original driver may have shifted and the pigment may have layered into deeper depths over time.

### Question 2. What was happening in your life around that time?

The most useful question in the consultation. Open. Lets the client surface the trigger themselves.

Listen for: pregnancy, contraceptive change, HRT, perimenopausal symptoms, period of stress or sleep disruption, a holiday with significant sun exposure, a new procedure or skincare regime.

### Question 3. Hormonal history.

Specific. Pregnancies, contraception (which formulations, when started, when stopped), HRT, cycle pattern, perimenopausal symptoms, thyroid history, family history of thyroid disease.

This is the most likely place the systemic driver shows up.

### Question 4. What have you already tried?

Their full skincare and treatment history. Past procedures. Past prescriptions. Past products. What they liked, what they didn't, and **what flared the pigment versus what didn't move it**.

This tells you their tolerance baseline and what's already been ruled in or out.

### Question 5. What does a typical week of sun and light exposure look like?

Indoor versus outdoor. Office near a window. Driving. Holiday frequency. Current SPF habit. Tinted versus clear. Applied once and forgotten, or reapplied. Phone screen time.

This is where you find out whether the SPF strategy will need rebuilding before any other layer.

### Question 6. What's your goal, and what would success look like?

The client's frame, not yours. "I want it gone" is different from "I want it not to flare again." "I want to look ten years younger" is different from "I want to feel comfortable without makeup." Knowing the target shapes the timeline conversation.

### Question 7. What's your timeline appetite?

Does the client need this resolved before a wedding in three months, or are they prepared for a year-long project? The mismatch between "want it gone next week" and the realistic twelve-week minimum is the single biggest source of disappointment in pigmentation work.

If a client's timeline is unrealistic, **say so before they pay**.

## The twelve-week timeline (and why twelve weeks is the minimum)

Most pigmentation responds on a melanocyte and keratinocyte turnover schedule that doesn't move faster than the underlying biology, regardless of the active applied. A reasonable expectation:

| Milestone | What to look for | What to say if you don't see it |
|---|---|---|
| **Week 4** | Surface texture calmer. Possibly slight reduction in surrounding redness. Pigment usually unchanged in tone, sometimes slightly more prominent (early turnover). | "What you're seeing now is the surface settling down. The pigment itself doesn't typically shift in the first four weeks. The early signal is the skin around the pigment, not the pigment itself." |
| **Week 8** | First visible reduction in pigment density for most epidermal cases. Melasma may remain stubborn. | "We're at the milestone where most clients see the first real shift. If we're not seeing it, we'll review whether the protocol is matched to the depth and adjust. That's a normal part of the process, not a failure." |
| **Week 12** | Substantial reduction in epidermal pigment for most responsive cases. Melasma visibly calmer. Plan for the next twelve weeks based on results. | "We've reached the first decision point. Based on what's moved, we'll either continue this plan, layer in a procedural step, or step back the actives if we're seeing a flare. Photo evidence will guide us." |

A client who knows this calendar in advance does not panic at week three when nothing has moved. They expect it. The plan holds.

## The photo protocol

Pigmentation outcomes are often invisible to the client without comparison photos. Their face changes day to day. The pigment doesn't dramatically disappear; it incrementally fades. Without photos, an objectively-improving client will still feel "nothing's working."

A consistent protocol:

- **Lighting.** Same light, same time of day, every visit. Daylight or a calibrated light box. No filters, no flash, no AI processing. If the clinic has a Visia or similar, use it consistently.
- **Framing.** Three angles minimum: full face, left 45 degrees, right 45 degrees. Same distance each time. Neutral expression.
- **Frequency.** Baseline, week four, week eight, week twelve, then quarterly.
- **Storage.** Securely stored per GDPR, with the client's written consent for clinical-record use.
- **Review with the client.** At each milestone, side-by-side comparison. The client sees the change you see.

Half of pigmentation client retention is photo evidence.

## The touchpoint cadence

A client on a twelve-week protocol shouldn't disappear between appointments. The clinic that holds the client through the slow middle is the clinic that gets the result.

A reasonable cadence:

- **Day 1 (consultation).** Plan written down. Photos taken. Treatment letter or email follow-up within 24 hours summarising the protocol and the timeline.
- **Day 7 check-in.** Short message (text, email, or app). "How is the new routine landing? Any irritation? Reply with a photo if anything's flaring."
- **Week 2.** Brief check-in. Adjust if needed.
- **Week 4 in-clinic visit.** First milestone photos and reassessment.
- **Week 6 check-in.** Mid-point support.
- **Week 8 in-clinic visit.** Second milestone.
- **Week 10 check-in.**
- **Week 12 in-clinic visit.** Decision point. Plan the next twelve weeks.

The check-ins between appointments are short, low-effort, high-trust touches. They keep the client engaged through the slow middle.

## The "your timeline is going to be longer" conversation

Some clients have presentations that simply will not respond on a twelve-week schedule. This needs to be said at consultation, not at week eight.

Candidates for the longer-timeline conversation:

- **Dermal or mixed pigment.** Six months minimum. Often layered with a procedural step.
- **Active melasma with current hormonal driver.** Three to six months for first stable result, with management ongoing.
- **Long-standing PIH on higher Fitzpatrick types.** Often six months for substantive improvement.
- **Combined presentations** (e.g. melasma plus solar lentigines plus PIH from past acne).

A useful script for these clients:

> What we're treating is going to take longer than the typical twelve weeks because [specific reason]. The first three months are the proof-of-concept phase. The next three to six months are where the substantive change happens. I want you to know that going in so you can plan around the timeline rather than feeling let down by it.

The clients who hear this honestly upfront are the clients who stay.
