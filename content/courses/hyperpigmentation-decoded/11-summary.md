## What NICE actually says about hyperpigmentation

NICE does not currently publish a single dedicated hyperpigmentation guideline. Different presentations are covered across different documents, and the framework you've learned reflects this:

- **Acne (NG198, 2021)** covers the post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation that follows acne management. Severity-grading, treatment escalation, and the conversation around long-term scarring and pigmentation risk are part of NICE-aligned acne care.
- **Melanoma and pigmented lesion guidance (NG12, NG7, and CKS pages)** sets the diagnostic priority: any pigmented lesion that doesn't fit a benign pattern needs to be screened against melanoma criteria before treatment.
- **CKS topics on melasma, lentigines, and PIH** describe primary-care management and signpost referral routes.

Translating this for private aesthetic practice:

1. **Screen first.** Any unusual lesion, atypical distribution, recent change in size or shape, irregular borders, asymmetry, or any of the standard ABCDE criteria — refer to GP or dermatology before treating.
2. **Match the protocol to the presentation.** The five-presentation framework in Module 1 mirrors how NICE-adjacent literature characterises hyperpigmentation subtypes.
3. **Document the rationale.** Each treatment decision should be defensible. The seven-question consultation in Module 8 is your defensibility.
4. **Refer where appropriate.** Suspected melanoma, suspected ochronosis, undiagnosed thyroid dysfunction, drug-induced pigmentation that needs medication review — these go up to GP or dermatology, not deeper into your own protocol.

## Documentation that defends a treatment plan

The same documentation discipline you would apply to acne or rosacea applies here:

- **Full medical history**, updated regularly, with hormonal life-events surfaced.
- **Treatment-specific consent forms**, signed and dated, with risks specific to hyperpigmentation included (rebound, PIH, ochronosis where prescribing applies).
- **Treatment record per visit**: products used, batch numbers, percentages, areas treated, times.
- **Photographic documentation** at baseline and milestones, with consent and secure storage.
- **Timeline expectations recorded** at the consultation and acknowledged by the client.

A practitioner who can produce these records when asked is in a categorically different defensibility position than one who can't.

## Knowledge check

Run these as honest self-checks. The aim is not a percentage score. It is to confirm you can act on the framework without prompting.

1. **A new client books in for "pigmentation". You see symmetrical brown-grey patches on the cheekbones in a 42-year-old who has been on combined HRT for two years.** What's the working presentation, the likely depth, the likely driver, and the first three things you ask?
2. **A 30-year-old Fitzpatrick III client has scattered discrete brown macules on the jaw following acne that cleared eighteen months ago.** What's the working presentation, the likely depth, and the first protocol you offer?
3. **A 56-year-old client wants "her age spots gone before a wedding in twelve weeks".** What's the working presentation, the likely depth, and the realistic plan?
4. **A 35-year-old Fitzpatrick V client comes in with extensive brown patches across the cheeks following a TCA peel six weeks ago.** What is the wrong move, what is the right first step, and what is the timeline?
5. **A melasma client three weeks post-peel returns with the pigment darker than baseline.** What are the five rebound triggers you check, in order, and what is the protocol for the next two weeks?

If you can answer all five without re-opening the modules, you have the framework.

## What you take into clinic

The version of you walking out of this course is the version that:

- Reads the face in the first thirty seconds.
- Asks the seven questions that surface the real driver.
- Sequences the topical layer for tolerance, not potency.
- Chooses the right modality for the presentation, and refuses the wrong one.
- Sets a twelve-week (or longer) timeline upfront.
- Holds the client through the slow middle with photo evidence and touchpoints.
- Recognises rebound early and corrects it without escalating.
- Refers when refer is the right answer.

That's a different consultation. That's a different result. That's a different reputation.
