Every detail of your client's experience, from their first DM to their follow-up aftercare, shapes whether they **return, refer, and remain loyal**. This module teaches practitioners how to design a seamless, emotionally positive journey that turns every client into a long-term advocate.

## The five stages of the client experience

### 1. Attraction, the first impression

Your client experience begins long before they book. Every piece of content, every DM, and every enquiry response shapes perception. Your marketing should mirror your in-clinic energy: **calm, knowledgeable, and trustworthy**.

- Does my content reflect the type of client experience I want to deliver?
- How quickly and warmly do I respond to enquiries?
- Does my tone of voice build trust and connection?

### 2. Booking, the connection point

From the moment they decide to book, your systems should feel smooth and professional. Booking should be **easy, clear, and reassuring**. Key elements to optimise:

- Simple online booking process
- Clear service descriptions
- Automated confirmation messages
- Deposit and cancellation policies communicated upfront

*Goal: reduce friction, increase trust.*

### 3. In-clinic experience, the transformation

This is where the magic happens. Every step of their visit should feel considered and professional:

- **Consultation process**, active listening, confidence, trust-building
- **Treatment delivery**, consistency, safety, care
- **Post-treatment wrap-up**, clear next steps and rebooking offer

*Goal: clients should leave thinking, "That felt seamless."*

### 4. Aftercare, the retention stage

Your job doesn't end when they leave the chair, it continues in your aftercare. A thoughtful follow-up system builds trust and reduces complications. Add these systems:

- Automated post-treatment message with aftercare details
- Follow-up check-in (24–48 hours post-visit)
- Rebooking reminder (2–4 weeks before next appointment)
- Personal thank-you message or card for new clients

*Goal: show clients that you care beyond the transaction.*

### 5. Relationship, the loyalty loop

This final stage is where you turn happy clients into **lifelong advocates**. Loyalty comes from consistency, recognition, and gratitude. Build long-term relationships through:

- Loyalty rewards or VIP programmes
- Exclusive seasonal offers for returning clients
- Birthday or anniversary messages
- Requesting reviews with personal follow-ups
- Inviting clients to share their transformations or testimonials

*Goal: create emotional connection and community, not just a service.*

## Improving the journey using CX design

CX (Client Experience) design is the process of creating interactions that are **easy, enjoyable, and emotionally positive** for clients. Improving the client journey involves applying structured design principles.

### Key CX design principles

1. **Client-centricity.** View everything from the client's perspective. Replace internal logic ("how we work") with external logic ("what the client needs").
2. **Empathy mapping.** Understand clients' thoughts, feelings, behaviours, and pain points. Helps you anticipate needs before clients express them.
3. **Simplicity and effort reduction.** Reduce friction in the client journey. Make processes quick, clear, and intuitive. High effort = low satisfaction, even if the service quality is good.
4. **Consistency.** Deliver the same level of service across all channels, phone, in-person, email, online. Avoid mixed messages or unpredictable experiences.
5. **Personalisation.** Tailor the experience to the individual client. Use client data, preferences, and past interactions to improve relevance and emotional connection.
6. **Emotional design.** Identify where the client may feel uncertain, stressed, confused, or frustrated. Add reassurance, clarity, or support at these emotional moments.
7. **Meet and exceed expectations.** Ensure baseline expectations are always met. Then add small "delight" elements that make the experience memorable.

Tools for improving the journey: client journey maps, pain-point analysis, Voice of Customer feedback, and continuous improvement and testing.

**Key point:** Treat your clinic as a system to be refined, not just a service to be delivered. That is the CEO mindset.

## Complaint handling, the HEART framework

The HEART Framework is a simple model focused on **empathy and reassurance**. It helps ensure clients feel heard, respected, and supported throughout the resolution process. By following each step, you can reduce tension, build trust, and turn negative experiences into positive outcomes.

- **H, Hear.** Listen fully, without interruption.
- **E, Empathise.** Show understanding of their frustration or feelings.
- **A, Apologise.** Offer a sincere apology, even if you personally did not cause the issue.
- **R, Resolve.** Take action to fix the problem or present options.
- **T, Thank.** Express appreciation for their feedback, complaints help improve service.

### Scenario, delayed service appointment

A client, Maria, arrives for her scheduled appointment and discovers there has been a mix-up, causing a 30-minute delay. She approaches the desk visibly frustrated.

- *H, Hear:* the staff member maintains eye contact and listens fully without interrupting.
- *E, Empathise:* "I can understand why you're frustrated, Maria. You planned ahead, and now this delay is affecting your schedule. I'm really sorry this has happened."
- *A, Apologise:* "I apologise for the mix-up and the inconvenience it's caused. We should have communicated this earlier."
- *R, Resolve:* "Let me fix this for you. I can prioritise your appointment and reduce the wait, or reschedule you for the earliest available time with a discount for the inconvenience. Which option works best for you?"
- *T, Thank:* "Thank you for your patience, Maria, and for bringing this to my attention. Your feedback helps us improve how we manage appointments."

*Outcome:* Maria feels listened to, respected, and taken care of. Even though the mistake caused frustration, the HEART framework helped turn a negative situation into a moment of trust and recovery.

## Weekly challenge, practical improvements

This week, initiate at least **two** of the below:

- Create a standardised pre- and post-care email for every treatment.
- Add an automated message that thanks new clients and invites reviews.
- Implement a "rebook before you leave" system with soft, confident language.
- Introduce a referral reward, even a small incentive goes far.
- Audit your consultation script, does it sound confident and intentional?
- Create one simple loyalty or membership system for repeat clients.
- Personalise your experience, use names, remember preferences, celebrate milestones.

Stay consistent and slowly build upon adding other practical elements as time allows.

**Best practice:** You can add pre- and post-care to your consent forms online. This results in the client having them all in one place, less hassle for the client and less admin for you.
