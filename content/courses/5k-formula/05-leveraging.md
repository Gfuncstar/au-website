This module is about **leveraging what you already have**, refining your treatment menu, creating a signature offer, pricing with confidence, and simplifying your services so clients say yes faster and your income grows sustainably.

## Identify your most profitable treatments

Not all treatments are created equal. Some generate high income with minimal effort; others require more time, product, or emotional energy than they're worth.

Ask yourself:

- How long does this treatment take vs. what it earns?
- What are my true costs (product, time, energy, follow-ups)?
- How do clients perceive the value of this service?
- Does this treatment attract the kind of clients I want long term?

### Align treatments with your niche and energy

If a service doesn't fit your niche, it creates noise in your business. Focus on treatments that align with your **core expertise**, your **client promise**, and your **personal energy**. For example: if your niche is natural facial rejuvenation, a laser or heavy cosmetic service might not align, even if it's profitable on paper. The right treatments should feel aligned with your purpose, values, and clinical style.

### Drainers vs. Drivers

Every treatment can be placed into one of two categories.

**Drivers** have high profit margins, consistent demand, are aligned with your niche, are energising to deliver, and often lead to rebookings or add-on sales.

**Drainers** have low margins or are time-intensive, have inconsistent demand, are emotionally or physically draining, are off-brand or outside your comfort zone, and rarely lead to repeat business.

Your goal is to do more of what drives you forward, and gradually reduce what doesn't. Highlight and promote your top three driver treatments, review your marketing to ensure it reflects these services, and reduce, reprice, or repackage your drainers over time.

**Best practice:** Focus on fewer, better services. The clearer your offer, the easier it is for clients to say yes.

## Create your signature service or package

Your signature service is the treatment or package that represents the **very best of what you do**, the one that delivers excellent results, aligns with your brand, and keeps clients returning again and again.

It's not just another service on your menu, it's your brand identity in action. Your signature offer positions you as a specialist, simplifies your marketing, and makes it easier for clients to understand what you're known for.

In aesthetics, your signature offer could be:

- A skin transformation programme (e.g. a 3-month course for skin rejuvenation)
- A confidence package combining injectables and skin health
- A membership or maintenance plan for consistent care

### Steps to build it

1. Identify the result clients want most (e.g. "clearer skin", "confidence in their appearance").
2. Choose the treatments that deliver that result best.
3. Decide on frequency or duration (e.g. 3 sessions over 12 weeks).
4. Add value, education, skincare recommendations, or a follow-up.
5. Name it clearly, choose something easy to remember and emotionally appealing.

### Naming and presenting your signature service

Your service name should communicate **result + feeling**, not product or ingredient. Avoid "3-Session Microneedling Course". Instead try: *"The Confidence Reboot, a personalised collagen-boosting programme for brighter, firmer skin."*

Presentation tips: use clear language your clients understand, describe benefits not just features, include a timeline or structure, and display it consistently across your platforms.

### Aligning profit and experience

Your signature package should be **both profitable and pleasant to deliver**. Check it aligns with the Three E's: does it reflect your skillset and best work (Expertise)? Do you enjoy performing it (Excitement)? Is it priced for healthy margins and sustainable profit (Economics)?

**Key point:** Be known for one thing done brilliantly. Mastery builds demand, and demand builds profit. This links back into creating your niche. If your clients can easily repeat what your package is and who it's for, your marketing is working.

## Design a clear client journey

In aesthetics, it's tempting to think "the more services I offer, the more clients I'll attract". In reality, the opposite is true. **Simplicity builds clarity. Clarity builds confidence. Confidence builds conversion.**

When your offers are focused, streamlined, and easy to understand, clients make faster decisions, and you build stronger demand for what you do best.

**Best practice:** Remember, what you remove can be just as powerful as what you add.

### The hidden cost of too many services

A long treatment list might look impressive, but it often leads to:

- Diluted marketing (it's hard to communicate clearly when you're promoting 15+ services)
- Inconsistent results (harder to master too many modalities)
- Lower profitability (some low-value treatments occupy prime booking time)
- Decision fatigue, both for clients and practitioners

## Price with confidence and intention

Pricing in aesthetics is more than numbers, it's a reflection of your **confidence, clarity, and brand positioning**. When you undercharge, you send a message that your expertise is negotiable. When you price intentionally, you attract clients who respect your skill, value your service, and commit to consistent care.

### The energy of pricing

Price is not just a figure, it's a signal. It communicates value, professionalism, and positioning. Low pricing says *"I'm still finding my feet."* Confident pricing says *"I know the value I deliver."* Pricing is an act of leadership, not fear.

Ask yourself:

- Do my prices reflect my expertise and results?
- Do they align with the level of care and experience I provide?
- Do I feel proud or apologetic when I share them?

If you feel resistance, it's rarely about numbers, it's about confidence.

### Understanding true profitability

**Formula:** (Treatment Price − Product Cost − Time Cost) = Gross Profit

*Example:* anti-wrinkle treatment at £250, product cost £45, 45 minutes of time. If your hourly value is £100, your time cost = £75. So: £250 − (£45 + £75) = **£130 profit**. Now consider: does that profit feel fair for your skill and experience? Numbers create clarity. Clarity creates confidence.

### The 3 P's of confident pricing

1. **Positioning**, where do you sit in the market? Are you boutique, luxury, or accessibility-focused? Your pricing should reflect your brand identity and client demographic.
2. **Perception**, what do clients believe they're investing in? You're not selling "anti-wrinkle", you're providing confidence, safety, and expertise.
3. **Profitability**, does your price support sustainable growth? Never price to survive, price to thrive.

### Communicating price with confidence

How you deliver your price is as important as the price itself.

**Do:**

- State your fee calmly and clearly
- Avoid over-explaining or apologising
- Use confident language: *"The investment for this treatment is £___."*

**Don't:**

- Justify your price mid-sentence
- Offer discounts automatically
- Undervalue your expertise to close a sale

When you hesitate, clients mirror that energy. When you're certain, they feel safe to say yes.

### Reviewing and raising prices

Your pricing should evolve with your skill, demand, and brand growth. Review prices every 6–12 months.

**When to raise prices:** you've invested in advanced training or equipment, you're fully booked or have a waiting list, or your results and reputation have grown.

**How to communicate an increase:** *"To reflect the continued investment in training, safety, and quality care, our treatment prices will be updated from [date]."* This reinforces professionalism, not apology.

## Weekly challenge, leverage your offers

These challenges are important because they push you to take **focused, strategic action** instead of getting stuck consuming information without implementing it. In aesthetics, small, intentional tweaks to your offers, pricing, and service structure can create massive shifts in profitability and clarity.

1. **Identify your top 3 "driver" treatments:** review your service menu and highlight the treatments that give you the highest profit, the strongest demand, and the best results. These are your business drivers, the ones that deserve 80% of your promotion and energy.
2. **Refine and promote ONE signature package:** choose one high-impact treatment (or combination) and turn it into a simple, results-led package. Give it a clear name, a defined outcome, and a structure clients can easily understand. This becomes your go-to offer for consistent income and clearer marketing.
3. **Remove or repackage your "drainers":** identify any low-profit, high-effort, or off-brand treatments and make one decision, remove, reprice, or repackage. Eliminating even one drainer creates space for your business to become more profitable and aligned.
