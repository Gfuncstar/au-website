Your expertise forms the **first pillar of your Clinical Core**. This is the foundation of what you do exceptionally well and what sets you apart from other practitioners in the industry.

Your expertise is not defined by how many treatments you offer, but by the quality and consistency of the results you deliver. This pillar invites you to take an honest look at where your true strengths lie, clinically, technically, and creatively.

When you understand your expertise, you're able to build a brand rooted in **confidence, clarity, and authority**. Clients trust practitioners who know exactly what they excel in, and this clarity becomes the first step in positioning yourself as a specialist.

Your niche is where your passion, skill, and profit meet. Use the **Three E's** (Expertise, Excitement and Economics) to find your sweet spot, the area where you can deliver exceptional results, enjoy your work, and build a sustainable business.

## Pillar One, Expertise

The first pillar is about recognising the value of what you already bring to the table. It's the foundation of your specialism, and when you own your expertise, you begin to attract clients who value and trust your abilities.

**Skills, certifications, and techniques**

- What formal training, specialist methods, or advanced techniques have you mastered?
- Where does your clinical knowledge outshine the average practitioner?

**Previous results**

- What results are you consistently proud of?
- Which before-and-afters get the strongest reactions or enquiries?
- Your past results are one of the strongest indicators of your true strengths.

**Treatments you deliver confidently and safely**

- Which services feel natural in your hands?
- Where do you feel calm, capable, and in control?
- Confidence paired with safety forms a core part of your professional identity.

**Services with high demand or profitability**

- Which treatments are clients already seeking you out for?
- Which ones contribute the most to your revenue?
- Understanding this helps you align your strengths with market demand.

**Best practice:** Clarity creates confidence. The more specific you are about who you serve and what you're known for, the faster your business will grow. Don't try to appeal to everyone, focus on the clients and treatments that truly light you up and deliver the best results. That's where your profit and purpose align.

## Pillar Two, Excitement

The second pillar of your Clinical Core is Excitement, and this is the part most practitioners overlook, yet it determines your long-term **fulfilment, creativity, and energy** in your business.

Excitement isn't a "nice to have". It's a strategic advantage.

When you genuinely enjoy the treatments you offer and the clients you work with, everything in your business becomes easier, your marketing flows better, your results improve, and you show up consistently without forcing yourself.

Within this pillar, explore the following:

**Treatment types that excite you**

- Which services light you up?
- Which treatments do you look forward to performing?
- The ones that energise you are usually the ones you perform best.

**Your ideal client's personality and goals**

- Who do you enjoy speaking to, educating, and treating?
- Who respects your expertise and values your approach?
- Your ideal client is not just about age or skin type, it's also about compatibility and shared expectations.

**What you'd still choose to do even without being paid**

- If money wasn't a factor, which treatments would you naturally gravitate toward?
- This helps you recognise your authentic passion, the work that feels effortless and meaningful.

**Where you feel most "in flow"**

- Which moments in your clinic make time pass quickly?
- Where do you feel the most creative, inspired, and confident?
- Your flow state is a powerful indicator of your natural strengths.

Pillar Two is about aligning your business with what feels good for *you*, not just what you can do, but what you *want* to do. When you honour your enjoyment, you create a business that's sustainable, inspiring, and uniquely yours. This is how your work becomes more than a service, it becomes an experience clients are drawn to.

## Pillar Three, Economics

The third pillar of your Clinical Core is Economics, the practical, financial backbone of your business. This is where you begin to make decisions that support **long-term growth, stability, and healthier income months**.

Many practitioners build their business around what they *can* do, not what is actually profitable or sustainable. But if you want consistent £5K+ months without burnout, you must understand the economics behind your services.

**Highest profit margin services.** Not all treatments generate the same return. Some require more product, time, or aftercare than others. Look closely at: product costs, treatment duration, your pricing, and how often clients return.

**Highest lifetime value clients.** Certain clients naturally rebook more, invest in packages, and refer others. Identifying these clients helps you build marketing and retention strategies around your most profitable client profiles.

**Low time-cost / high price services.** Some treatments allow you to charge well while maintaining a manageable workload. These services reduce burnout, increase profitability, and create space for better client care. Your goal is to build a business that rewards your skill without exhausting your energy.

**Opportunities for memberships or recurring revenue.** Stable income is created through consistency, not one-off appointments. Look for treatments that can be turned into skin journeys, maintenance memberships, seasonal top-up plans, or monthly or quarterly care packages. These create predictable income and strengthen client loyalty.

Pillar Three ensures your business model supports both your lifestyle and your financial goals. When your services are aligned with strong economics, you naturally reduce stress, improve cash flow, and build a business that grows *with* you, not at the expense of you.

**Key point:** Pillar Three empowers you to move from "busy" to profitable, and from working hard to working smart.

## Drainers vs. Drivers

To simplify, every treatment can be placed into one of two categories:

**Drivers:** high profit margins, consistent demand, aligned with your niche, energising and satisfying to deliver, often lead to rebookings or add-on sales.

**Drainers:** low margins or time-intensive, inconsistent demand, emotionally or physically draining, off-brand or outside your comfort zone, rarely lead to repeat business.

Your goal is to do more of what drives you forward, and gradually reduce what doesn't. You don't need to overhaul your entire service menu, just refine it. Highlight and promote your top three driver treatments, review your marketing to ensure it reflects these top services, and reduce, reprice, or repackage your drainers over time. Build your brand reputation around what you do best.

**Best practice:** Focus on fewer, better services. The clearer your offer, the easier it is for clients to say yes.

## Weekly challenge, unlock clarity, brand, and profit

This week is all about getting clear, visible, and profitable, building the foundation for your £5K+ months. These simple actions will help you understand your niche, refine your brand, and focus on what truly grows your aesthetics business.

1. **Complete your Three E's audit**, list your top treatments under Expertise (what you're best at), Excitement (what lights you up), and Economics (what pays you well). Highlight where all three overlap, that's your profitable niche focus.
2. **Craft your niche statement:** *"I help [ideal client] achieve the [desired result] through [your signature approach]."* Keep it visible, this becomes the foundation for your brand message and marketing.
3. **Refine your visibility:** audit your Instagram bio, website, or booking page. Does it clearly reflect your niche and what you offer? Update your wording or visuals to show professionalism and personality.
4. **Choose your three brand words**, e.g. natural, empowering, trusted. Use them to guide how you speak, post, and design, consistency builds recognition and trust.
5. **Review your treatment list:** identify your top 3 income-generating or high-demand services. Create one simple signature package that combines your most popular, results-driven treatments for higher perceived value and profitability.
6. **Post one educational or personal content piece** that showcases your expertise. Share your story, your philosophy, or a transformation to connect authentically with your audience.

**Important:** For some people, posting on social media can be daunting and uncomfortable. However, social media is your shop front and it is typically where first impressions are made. It is important that you commit to showcasing your expertise and your skills on whichever platform you choose. Everyone has their own boundaries with this. Set yours and get posting, but make sure you are posting.
