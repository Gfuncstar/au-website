Acne is one of the most common skin conditions in the UK, yet it is also one of the most misunderstood. Throughout this course, we have explored acne as more than a surface-level concern, recognising it as a chronic inflammatory skin condition that requires informed assessment, evidence-based treatment, and compassionate client care.

You have developed a strong understanding of:

- What acne is and how it develops within the pilosebaceous unit
- The four key pathophysiological drivers, excess sebum production, abnormal keratinisation, microbial imbalance, and inflammation
- The role of the immune response and why inflammation is central to acne progression
- UK-specific classification of acne severity and how this guides treatment and referral
- Causes, risk factors, risks, and long-term complications, including scarring and psychological impact
- At-home treatment strategies that form the foundation of acne management
- In-clinic treatments and how to use them ethically, safely, and effectively
- When and why referral is essential, aligned with NICE and NHS guidance
- How to apply knowledge through real-life UK case studies and reflective practice

This course has emphasised that acne management is **not about quick fixes or aggressive treatment**, but about **long-term skin health, education, and trust**. Effective acne care requires patience, consistency, realistic expectations, and an understanding that every client's skin and experience is unique.

By aligning practice with NICE guidance, you are equipped to work confidently within your scope, protect client wellbeing, and communicate clearly with other healthcare professionals when escalation is needed.
