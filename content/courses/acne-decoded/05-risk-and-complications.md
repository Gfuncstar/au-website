Acne carries both **short-term risks and long-term complications**. Persistent inflammation, barrier damage, pigmentation changes, scarring, and psychological distress are all possible outcomes, particularly when acne is untreated or poorly managed. Early, informed, and appropriate intervention is essential to minimise long-term harm.

The risks of acne relate to what may occur during the active phase of the condition, particularly when acne is severe, persistent, or improperly managed.

![earlier intervention reduces every downstream risk.](/illustrations/acne-decoded/05-risk-and-complications/01-shield.png)

## 1. Risk of persistent inflammation

- Ongoing inflammation damages surrounding skin tissue
- Repeated flare-ups prolong the inflammatory cycle
- Inflammation may be present even before visible lesions appear

**Why this matters:** Chronic inflammation increases the likelihood of pigmentation changes, delayed healing, and scarring.

## 2. Risk of skin barrier damage

Acne-prone skin is often over-treated.

Common contributors:

- Excessive cleansing
- Over-exfoliation
- Inappropriate active ingredient layering

This can lead to:

- Increased transepidermal water loss (TEWL)
- Sensitivity and irritation
- Worsening acne rather than improvement

## 3. Risk of post-inflammatory pigmentation

- Inflammation stimulates melanocytes
- Results in dark marks (post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation)
- More common in deeper skin tones but can affect all skin types

**Key point:** Pigmentation can persist long after active acne has resolved.

## 4. Risk of physical scarring

- Deep inflammation increases dermal damage
- Nodules and cysts are particularly high-risk
- Picking, squeezing, or aggressive extraction increases risk

**Important:** Once scarring forms, it is permanent and difficult to treat.

## 5. Psychological and emotional risk

Acne can significantly affect:

- Self-esteem
- Social confidence
- Body image

These effects can occur at any age and at any acne severity level. Emotional distress may increase stress hormones, further worsening acne.
