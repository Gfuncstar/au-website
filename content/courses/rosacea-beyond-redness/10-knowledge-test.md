This section is designed as a reflective knowledge check. Work through the questions below using your own knowledge and professional judgement, drawing on the material covered in the previous chapters.

Practitioners are expected to complete all questions honestly, based on their own knowledge and professional judgement. Suggested answers are intended for reference purposes only — the value lies in the thinking, not the answer key.

## How to approach the test

- Set aside 15–20 minutes of uninterrupted time
- Answer in your own words rather than copying from the course
- Where you're unsure, return to the relevant chapter and re-read before answering
- Use the questions to identify gaps in your confidence as much as your knowledge

## Suggested topic areas to self-test

Use the following prompts as a structured self-review covering the spine of the course:

1. Define rosacea in your own words. How does it differ from acne vulgaris?
2. Name the four physiological systems that become dysregulated in rosacea.
3. Describe what happens to the facial vasculature in repeated flushing episodes.
4. What is the role of LL-37 and Toll-like receptor 2 (TLR-2) in rosacea inflammation?
5. Why is barrier repair foundational, rather than corrective?
6. Explain the difference between a trigger and a cause, using *Demodex* as an example.
7. List six common rosacea triggers a client should track in a flare diary.
8. What are the four key clinical patterns to recognise during visual assessment?
9. Which features must always prompt a referral to a GP or dermatologist?
10. What is rhinophyma, and which subtype of rosacea does it sit within?
11. Describe the foundation phase of the NICE-aligned management pathway.
12. Which in-clinic treatments are appropriate for stable rosacea, and which are contraindicated?
13. Why is daily SPF a non-negotiable part of homecare advice?
14. In a reactive / neurogenic case, what is the primary treatment goal?
15. What is the most common practitioner mistake when working with sensitised rosacea skin?

**Best practice:** When you've finished, mark any questions where you hesitated and revisit those chapters. The goal of this test is not a score — it's confidence at the consultation chair.
