Rosacea is one of the most common chronic inflammatory skin conditions seen in aesthetic practice — and one of the most often mismanaged. Throughout this course, we've explored rosacea as more than a redness concern, recognising it as a **neurovascular and immune-mediated condition** that requires informed assessment, evidence-based treatment, and compassionate client care.

You have developed a strong understanding of:

- What rosacea is, and why it sits at the intersection of vascular, neurogenic, immune and barrier dysfunction
- How rosacea presents along a **spectrum of phenotypes** rather than as a single disease
- The central role of barrier integrity and microbiome balance in stabilising the condition
- How to assess rosacea clinically — through history, observation, pattern recognition and scope-of-practice judgement
- The interacting causes and triggers that drive flares
- The long-term complications of unmanaged inflammation, including phymatous and ocular involvement
- A NICE-aligned management plan covering foundation phase, appropriate in-clinic treatments, and what to defer
- Practical homecare advice and the messages that change client outcomes
- How to apply your knowledge through three real-world case studies

This course has emphasised that rosacea management is **not about quick fixes or aggressive treatment**, but about **long-term skin health, education, and trust**. Effective rosacea care requires patience, consistency, realistic expectations, and a genuine understanding that every client's skin and experience is unique.

By aligning your practice with NICE guidance, you are equipped to work confidently within your scope, protect client wellbeing, and communicate clearly with other healthcare professionals when escalation is needed.

**Key point:** Calm skin first. Consistency matters more than intensity. In rosacea, patience is a treatment.
