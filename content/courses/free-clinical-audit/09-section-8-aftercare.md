Aftercare is the section that converts treatment plans into outcomes the clinic can stand behind. It is also the section where the difference between "we usually do" and "we always document" becomes visible. The standard is simple: every treatment, written aftercare, every time, recorded.

## Checklist

- **Written aftercare** for every treatment, explained at the appointment AND documented in the client's record
- **Follow-up plan** recorded per client (timing, mode of contact, what's being checked)
- **Post-treatment communication logged** (phone call, message, email) in the same record as the treatment itself
- **Treatment-specific aftercare templates** that match what you actually advise (not generic boilerplate)

## Notes

- "Verbal aftercare only" does not exist as a defensible position. If it isn't written, it didn't happen, from the regulator's perspective. The aftercare leaflet is the receipt for the conversation.
- Follow-up is what turns one treatment into a clinical relationship. A clinic that follows up at 24 hours, 7 days, and (where appropriate) 6 weeks demonstrates a level of care that complaint reviewers and indemnity insurers both notice.
- The follow-up needs to be in the client's record. A WhatsApp on your personal phone is not in the client's record. The clinical-records software entry is.
- Treatment-specific means the aftercare for filler is different from the aftercare for skinboosters, which is different again from the aftercare for microneedling. Generic boilerplate is the thing inspectors find unconvincing.

## Where to source

- Manufacturer aftercare instructions (request templates from each supplier)
- The JCCP Code of Practice for aftercare standards
- Your indemnity insurer's aftercare templates
- Your clinical-records software's aftercare module (most have one)

If your aftercare is verbal, or if your follow-up is genuinely consistent but not yet documented, you have a quick-win section. The work is mostly bringing what you already do into writing, not changing what you do.
