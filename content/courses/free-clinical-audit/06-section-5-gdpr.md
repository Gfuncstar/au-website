Every other section of this audit produces personal data: medical history, consent, treatment records, photographs, complaint logs. GDPR is the spine that holds all of it together. Inspectors and complaint reviewers do not always look here first, but when they do, the gap is usually large.

## Checklist

- **Registered with the [Information Commissioner's Office](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/data-protection-fee/self-assessment/)** as a data controller (annual fee, ~£40 to £60 for small clinics)
- **Privacy policy** in place, accessible to clients, covering what data you collect, why, where it's stored, who can see it, how long you keep it
- **Secure client record storage** with access controls (password-protected software, locked cabinet, or both)
- **Data retention policy** defined and applied consistently (typical: ten years post last contact for adult clinical records, longer for minors)

## Notes

- ICO registration is a yearly self-assessment. Most aesthetic clinics fall into Tier 1 (small organisations), with the fee currently around £40. Failing to register is a strict-liability offence; the ICO publishes the offending clinics by name.
- A privacy policy is not boilerplate. It must reflect the data you actually collect. If you take photographs and don't mention photography in your privacy policy, the policy is not compliant.
- Secure storage is about access, not just encryption. A clinical record on a shared family computer with no separate login is not secure storage, even if the file itself is encrypted.
- Retention is a discipline, not a one-off statement. Set a recurring annual review (e.g. every January) to delete records that have passed their retention date.

## Where to source

- ICO self-assessment tool at ico.org.uk
- ICO sample privacy policy templates (free)
- Your clinical-records software's GDPR documentation
- The [JCCP](https://aestheticsunlocked.co.uk/standards/jccp) Code of Practice section on data handling

If you are not currently registered with the ICO, register before you continue with the rest of this audit. It is a fifteen-minute fix, and it is the only item on this checklist where being unregistered creates a personal liability.
