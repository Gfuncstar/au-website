You've worked through eight sections. The closing check is what tells you whether the audit holds together end to end, or whether the gaps you ticked are scattered enough to undermine the whole.

This is the test an inspector or a complaint reviewer is doing in their head when they walk in. Run it on yourself.

## The three questions that decide it

### 1. Can you produce every document on demand?

Pick three random clients from the last twelve months. For each one, can you produce, inside ten minutes:

- Their full medical history (current, not stale)
- Their treatment-specific consent form (signed and dated)
- Their treatment records (with batch numbers)
- Their photographs (with photography consent)
- Their aftercare and follow-up trail

If yes for all three clients, this section is in good shape. If no for any of them, the gap is in your records system, not in this client. Fix the system.

### 2. Is the paperwork consistent across clients?

Compliance gaps don't usually fail because the documents are missing entirely. They usually fail because the documents are inconsistent, present for some clients, absent for others, full for some treatments, partial for others.

Ask yourself:

- Does every client have the same level of documentation for the same treatment?
- Does every treatment in your service list have its own consent template?
- Does your aftercare leaflet match what you actually say at the chair?

Inconsistency is the thing complaint reviewers notice fastest. A clinic with imperfect-but-consistent paperwork survives scrutiny better than a clinic with perfect paperwork on some clients and gaps on others.

### 3. Does the paperwork reflect the treatments you actually perform?

This is the most common failure point.

- Your insurance certificate names treatments. Are all the treatments you currently perform on it?
- Your COSHH file names products. Are all the products in clinic on it?
- Your consent forms name procedures. Are all the procedures in your service list covered?
- Your privacy policy describes data. Does it cover photography, automated reminders, and any third-party tools you use?

Most clinics' paperwork lags six to twelve months behind their actual practice. The audit closes that gap.

## What this audit was designed to do

You now have:

- A complete picture of the eight compliance areas every English aesthetic clinic is expected to evidence
- A short list of the gaps that matter most for your specific clinic
- A defensible position to work from, not a vague unease

You haven't fixed the gaps yet. You've named them. That is itself the work most clinics never do, and it is what separates a defensible practice from a hopeful one.
