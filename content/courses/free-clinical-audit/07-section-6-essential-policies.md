Policies are not a regulatory ornament. They are how a clinic demonstrates it has thought through the foreseeable risks before they happen. An inspector's first request is usually for the policy file. A clinic that can produce one within minutes is treated very differently to one that has to "look for it".

The good news: policy templates are free, the writing is mostly tailoring, and the file builds itself once you commit to one Saturday morning.

## Checklist

- **Complaints procedure** in writing, accessible to clients, with named owner and timescales
- **Accident / incident log** in place (whether or not it has any entries yet)
- **Safeguarding policy** covering children and vulnerable adults (where applicable to your client base)
- **Lone working policy** if you ever see clients without another adult on the premises
- **Health & Safety policy** as a one-page parent document tying the COSHH, fire, and treatment-specific risk assessments together
- **Staff policies** covering employment, contracts, GDPR responsibilities (where applicable)

## Notes

- A complaints procedure is often the deciding document in a disputed case. It should name a complaints owner, give a written response timescale (typically ten working days for acknowledgement), and signpost the client to the appropriate professional regulator if the matter cannot be resolved. The [JCCP](https://aestheticsunlocked.co.uk/standards/jccp) and [GMC](https://www.gmc-uk.org) and [NMC](https://www.nmc.org.uk) all publish escalation guidance.
- An accident / incident log doesn't mean accidents have occurred. It is the evidence that you have somewhere to record one if it does, with the right fields (date, client, treatment, what happened, immediate response, follow-up, lessons learned).
- Safeguarding applies wherever you treat anyone under 18 or any vulnerable adult. If your client base is exclusively self-presenting adults, a brief policy stating that scope is still expected.
- Lone working covers what you do if a client deteriorates while you are alone with them. It is not optional for sole practitioners; it is the policy that protects you as much as the client.

## Where to source

- The JCCP Code of Practice template library
- ACAS for staff-policy templates
- Your indemnity insurer's policy library (most provide free)
- Your local authority's environmental health team for safeguarding guidance

The policy file is one of the easiest gaps to close. Most aesthetic clinics can build the entire file in a single morning using free templates, lightly tailored to their actual practice.
