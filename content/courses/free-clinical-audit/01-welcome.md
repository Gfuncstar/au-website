Welcome.

If you're here, it tells me something already. You don't just want your clinic to feel right. You want it to **be defensible**.

This audit is the one I run against my own clinic. I've distilled it down so any English aesthetic practitioner can use it the same way I do. It's not theoretical, it's not a marketing tool, and it's not a course about regulation. It is the working spreadsheet of a working clinic, made plain.

## What the audit is

Eight sections. Each section is a short, honest, yes/no checklist that maps your current paperwork against what an inspector, an insurer, or a complaint reviewer would expect to see. At the end of section eight, a closing inspection-readiness check confirms the audit holds together end to end.

## What the audit is not

- It is not legal advice. It is a self-audit a practitioner uses to triage their own gaps.
- It is not a substitute for the [JCCP standards](https://www.jccp.org.uk) or for the incoming national licensing scheme. It is the practical layer underneath both.
- It is not a perfection test. You will tick "no" against some items. That is what the audit is for.

## How to use it

Work through each section honestly. Tick yes only where you can produce the document on demand, in a form that would survive scrutiny. If you cannot produce it inside ten minutes, tick no.

When you finish, you will have:

- A clear picture of where you stand
- A short list of the gaps that matter most
- A defensible position to work from, not a vague unease

There is no judgement here. Most practitioners I teach come in with at least three gaps. The point of the audit is to surface those gaps so you can close them on your own terms, before someone else surfaces them for you.

## Before you start

You will need rough access to:

- Your insurance certificate
- Your local-authority Special Treatments Licence (if applicable)
- Your consultation, consent, treatment-record and aftercare templates
- Your ICO registration
- Your written policies (complaints, safeguarding, lone working, health & safety)
- Your stock log

If any of that is uncomfortable to find, that is itself useful information. Note it and keep going.
