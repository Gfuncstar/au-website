Every product you put into a client links directly back to the treatment record in Section 3. Stock control is what makes that link defensible. If a manufacturer issues a recall, or a complaint reviewer asks "where did this product come from", you need an answer within minutes. That answer lives in this section's checklist.

## Checklist

- **Products sourced from approved, regulated suppliers only** (genuine UK distributors, not overseas grey-market resellers)
- **Batch numbers recorded per client treatment**, traceable both ways (client to batch, batch to clients)
- **Expiry dates monitored** with a written rotation procedure (first-in-first-out)
- **Storage conditions followed** for every product (temperature, light protection, security)
- **Stock tracking system in place** (spreadsheet, software, or paper log, anything you can produce on demand)

## Notes

- The MHRA Yellow Card scheme requires you to be able to identify which clients received which batch in the event of a product safety alert. Without batch records per treatment, you cannot comply with that obligation.
- "Approved supplier" means a supplier authorised to distribute the product in the UK. Check the supplier's MHRA registration where applicable. A discount on a grey-market import is not a saving once you are asked to demonstrate provenance.
- Cold-chain products (toxin in particular) require temperature logs. A clinic-fridge thermometer with a daily check is the minimum. Most clinical-fridge thermometers now log automatically; consider one if you don't already.
- Expiry monitoring needs to be a system, not vigilance. A dated rotation sheet, or a spreadsheet that alerts on approaching expiry, is the difference between catching a near-expiry product on Tuesday and discovering it during a treatment on Friday.

## Where to source

- Manufacturer / distributor approved-supplier confirmation (request in writing)
- The MHRA Yellow Card scheme guidance for adverse-event reporting
- Cold-chain temperature loggers from any clinical-supplier catalogue
- The JCCP Code of Practice section on stock and supply

If you cannot produce a list of every product currently in clinic, with batch number and expiry, in under five minutes, this is a section worth working on this week.
