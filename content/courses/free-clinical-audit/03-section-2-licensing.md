Licensing is the section the law has been moving fastest on. England's Special Treatments Licence regime sits with local authorities today; the national licensing scheme under the [Health and Care Act 2022](https://www.gov.uk/government/consultations/licensing-of-non-surgical-cosmetic-procedures) is rolling out alongside it through 2024 to 2026. This section maps your current standing against both.

## Checklist

- **Special Treatments Licence** — checked against your local authority, obtained where required, conditions read and followed
- **Awareness of the incoming national licensing scheme** — you have read the DHSC consultation outcome and you know whether your services fall under the highest-risk, medium-risk, or lower-risk band
- **Practitioner-licensing readiness** — qualifications evidence, indemnity, and CPD record ready for inspection
- **Premises-licensing readiness** — clinic premises documentation organised, separate from the practitioner file
- **Treatment-specific insurance** in place (e.g. Hamilton Fraser, Cosmetic Insure) and matching the treatments you actually perform
- **Certificates and CPD evidence** matched to the procedures listed on your insurance certificate

## Notes

- The Special Treatments Licence is administered by your local council. The exact name varies (some councils call it "Special Treatments Licensing", some "Skin-Piercing Licensing"). If you're unsure whether yours is required, the council's environmental health team is the right first call.
- The incoming national licensing scheme separates **practitioner licences** from **premises licences**. Both will be required for in-scope procedures. Treat them as two parallel files, not one.
- Insurance certificates list procedures by name. If your certificate names "dermal fillers" but you also perform polynucleotides or skin boosters, those need adding. A complaint reviewer will check this within minutes.

## Where to source

- Local authority website, environmental health or licensing team
- DHSC consultation outcome at gov.uk
- [JCCP](https://www.jccp.org.uk) for national-scheme readiness guidance
- Your insurance broker (annual review, request a written confirmation that all your treatments are covered)

If your insurance certificate hasn't been reviewed against your actual treatment list in the last twelve months, that is your single most urgent gap.
