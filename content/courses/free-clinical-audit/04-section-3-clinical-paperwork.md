Every defensible treatment plan rests on four documents: medical history, consent, treatment record, and clinical photography with consent. If any of the four is weak, the plan collapses under scrutiny. This is the section regulators and complaint reviewers check first.

## Checklist

- **Full medical history** recorded for every client and updated regularly (not "once at intake")
- **Treatment-specific consent forms** signed and dated by the client, with the specific risks of the named treatment outlined in writing
- **Treatment record per visit** capturing product, batch, dosage, treatment area, and date
- **Before/after photography** with explicit written consent, secure storage, and a record-link back to the client file

## Notes

- Medical history is a living document. A client who started with you two years ago needs updates for new medications, pregnancy, recent surgery, or new diagnoses. A single intake form from year one is not a current medical history.
- Treatment-specific consent means the consent form names the treatment, the product class, and the specific risks of that treatment. A generic "I consent to aesthetic treatment" form is not consent.
- Batch numbers per visit are non-negotiable. They are how you trace a product back to the supplier if a manufacturer recall happens, and they are how an insurer or coroner reconstructs what happened in the rare worst-case event.
- Photography requires its own consent layer: storage location, retention period, who else can see them, and whether they may be used for training or marketing. The CCTV-style "all photos for any purpose" consent is not lawful under GDPR.

## Where to source

- Royal College of Nursing template library
- The [JCCP / CPSA Code of Practice](https://www.jccp.org.uk) for consent standards
- Indemnity insurer's recommended consent forms
- Your secure clinic-management software (Pabau, Aesthetic Nurse Software, etc.) for record templates

If you can't produce a single client's full record (medical history + consent + treatment record + photographs) inside three minutes, this is your most urgent section.
