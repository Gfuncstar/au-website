Health & Safety is the section most clinics quietly skip, partly because the language sounds factory-floor rather than clinic-floor, and partly because the documents tend to live in five different places. The two most common gaps are an out-of-date COSHH file and a missing fire risk assessment.

Walk through the questions below and tick yes only where you can produce the document on demand.

## Checklist

- **COSHH risk assessments** — completed, accessible, reviewed at least annually
- **Dermal fillers and injectables** included within COSHH (commonly missed)
- **Safety Data Sheets (SDS)** held for every product currently in clinic
- **Fire risk assessment** in place, dated, and reviewed
- **Treatment-specific risk assessments** for the procedures you actually perform
- **Infection-control risk assessment** sitting alongside the procedures it covers

## Notes

- COSHH is the [Control of Substances Hazardous to Health Regulations 2002](https://www.hse.gov.uk/coshh/). The Health and Safety Executive publishes free templates that are perfectly acceptable starting points.
- Safety Data Sheets are issued by manufacturers. If you don't have an SDS on file for a product, the manufacturer is required to send you one on request. Email them today; the file builds itself fast once you ask.
- A "treatment-specific risk assessment" doesn't need to be a 20-page document. A single page per treatment covering the foreseeable risks, mitigations, and your escalation path is sufficient.

## Where to source

- HSE template library at hse.gov.uk
- Manufacturer SDS by direct request to each supplier
- The fire risk assessment template from your local fire and rescue service (most publish one)

If three or more items above came back as "no", flag this section as a priority gap. None of these documents are legally optional, and all of them are inexpensive to put right.
