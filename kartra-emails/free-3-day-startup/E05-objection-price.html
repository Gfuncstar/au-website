Is £1,199 a lot for a business programme?

Hi {first_name},

A question that comes up: is £1,199 a lot to spend on a business programme?

Two ways to answer that.

The first is a comparison. Equivalent one-to-one business mentoring with an aesthetics-specialist consultant runs £200 to £400 per hour. The 5K+ Formula™ is twelve weeks, twelve modules, lifetime access, including the live touch-points. Adding £350 to a single month's revenue through tighter pricing and signature offers covers the programme back inside one quarter. Most practitioners who finish the work and apply it report monthly revenue shifts that recoup the fee in the first month.

The second is more honest. Most practitioners aren't underpaid because they're undertrained clinically. They're underpaid because the business muscle was never built. Pricing held flat for three years. Discounting to fill quiet weeks. Marketing that competes on price because the niche was never decided. The diary owns the practitioner instead of the practitioner owning the diary.

The 5K+ Formula™ is what closes that gap. It is the system I built my own clinic, Visage Aesthetics, on, the clinic that won Best Non-Surgical Aesthetics Clinic 2026 (Essex). I'm not going to pretend the framework will work for someone who isn't prepared to do the weekly business rituals. I will say that for the practitioners who do, the work compounds in ways that make the fee feel small inside a year.

You don't need a thousand new clients. You need to keep the right ones, charge the price the work is actually worth, and run the clinic on numbers you trust.
