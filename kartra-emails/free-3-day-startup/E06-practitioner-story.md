# E6 — Practitioner outcome story (Day 11)

**Subject:** `What changed when she stopped chasing every client`

**Preheader:** `One practitioner's quiet shift, after the 5K+ Formula.`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

A practitioner I worked with last year told me something that
stuck.

Before the programme, she was running a clinic that looked
successful from the outside. The diary was full, the Saturday list
was rammed, the social posts were getting saved. From the inside,
the maths was grim. Average treatment value flat. Retention
mediocre. Three days a week she was doing treatments she didn't
even particularly enjoy, for clients who'd shop elsewhere the
moment a discount appeared. Her income had not moved in three
years.

Twelve weeks into the programme, she wrote to me with the
honest read-out.

She'd dropped two treatments off her menu. She'd raised her prices
on three. She'd built two signature offers around the niche she'd
finally let herself claim, perimenopausal skin, the area of work
she actually loved. She had a one-line brand statement that made
half her old marketing feel embarrassing in retrospect, in the
good way. The diary was quieter, on paper. The bank account was
not.

What she said most clearly: the work hadn't got harder. The
clients she now saw weren't more demanding, they were less. The
practice felt lighter because the decisions had already been
made.

That's the shift the programme is built to produce. Not more
hours. Better hours. Numbers you can trust, and a clinic that
finally pays you what the work is worth.
