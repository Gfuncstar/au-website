Hi {first_name},

A confession. For my first few years running a clinic, I confused busy with profitable.

The diary was full. The Saturday list was rammed. I was tired in a way I assumed meant the work was paying me. Then I sat down with the actual numbers, retention, average treatment value, the proportion of revenue that came from the right clients, and realised my busiest months were quietly my least profitable ones. The clients I was running myself ragged for weren't the ones keeping the clinic alive. The clinic was being held up by a quiet group of retained clients I'd been giving the least attention to.

That was the moment I built the weekly read-out. Five numbers I look at every Monday morning. They take fifteen minutes to update and they tell me, ruthlessly, whether the previous week was busy-good or busy-bad, and what to change first.

Day 3 walks you through the read-out and the questions it answers. It's the simplest piece of business work most clinics will ever do, and it's the one that decides whether the clinic pays you or just employs you.
