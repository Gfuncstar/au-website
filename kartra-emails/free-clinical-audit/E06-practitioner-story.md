# E6 — Practitioner outcome story (Day 10)

**Subject:** `What changed once the audit gaps were closed`

**Preheader:** `One practitioner's quiet shift, after the RAG Pathway.`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

A practitioner I taught last year told me something that stuck.

Before the Pathway, she'd taken the audit and given herself a long
amber list. The work was honest, the gaps were clear, but the
list sat on her laptop for three months without anything moving.
Every Monday she meant to start, and every Monday a client query
or a stock issue or a consultation took priority. The audit had
shown her what was missing. It hadn't given her the structure to
close it.

After the Pathway, she wrote to me and said the most useful thing
hadn't been any single module. It had been the rhythm. Four weeks
of structured work where the consent templates landed in week
two, the complication management plan landed in week three, the
ASA-safe content framework landed in week four. Each one slotted
in at a pace she could actually manage alongside running a clinic,
because the work was already structured for her.

By the end of the four weeks, the amber list had collapsed to two
remaining items, both already in motion. The clinic that had been
running on hope was now running on documentation. The consent
forms were current, the photography was logged against the
treatment record, the complaints procedure was no longer just in
her head, and the audit she'd done as a free taster three months
earlier had become her quarterly self-check.

That's the shift the Pathway is built to produce. Not more rules.
Less anxiety. The audit list closed, on paper, in four weeks.
