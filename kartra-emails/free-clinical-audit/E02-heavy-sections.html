If you've started the audit, you'll already have a feel for which sections feel comfortable and which ones feel exposing.

There are two sections most practitioners want to skim past, and they're the two where the real work sits.

The first is Section 3, Core clinical paperwork. Consultation, consent, treatment record, photography. The four-document foundation underneath every defensible plan. Most clinics have each of these in some form. Almost no clinics have all four linked together, dated, batch-numbered, and producible on demand for the same client across a year of treatments. That gap, the distance between "I have it somewhere" and "I can produce it in five minutes," is where the audit usually lands hardest.

The second is Section 6, Essential policies and documents. Complaints procedure, accident log, lone working, safeguarding, health and safety. The policies inspectors and insurers expect to find on day one and that most clinics intend to write someday. The audit will tell you, ruthlessly, which ones aren't there.

Both sections are designed to be uncomfortable. That's the point. The audit isn't there to confirm what you already know; it's there to surface what you've been postponing.

Take the time on these two if you haven't already.
