## Body

Hi {first_name},

A question that comes up: is £79 a lot to spend on one condition?

Two ways to answer that.

The first is to count clients. If your client base sees acne even
occasionally, the course pays for itself the first time you place a
client on the right driver-led plan instead of the wrong
generic one. One client kept, instead of churning out at week six
when their skin worsens, and the course has earned its keep.

The second is more honest. Acne is the condition practitioners
quietly dread. It's the one where outcomes are unpredictable, where
the client emotional load is highest, and where a bad result is
visible to everyone in their life. Most practitioners build a
career trying to avoid acne clients. The ones who learn to handle
acne well end up with a quiet stream of referrals, because nobody
else will touch them.

You don't need a thousand acne clients. You need to be the
practitioner in your area whose name comes up when a different
practitioner's plan didn't work.
