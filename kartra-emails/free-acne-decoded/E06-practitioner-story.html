Hi {first_name},

A practitioner I taught last year told me something that stuck.

Before the course, she was treating acne the way she'd been trained to. Cleanser, exfoliant, retinoid, hope. Some clients did well. About a third came back at week six worse than they started, and she didn't know why. She started turning down acne consults.

After the course, she wrote to me and said the most useful thing hadn't been the protocols. It had been the consultation framework. She'd stopped opening with "what are you using on your skin" and started opening with "tell me about your last six months." Sleep, stress, contraceptive history, recent antibiotics, gut symptoms.

The first time she mapped that history against what she was seeing on the skin, she said it felt like the room got quieter.

She hasn't turned a single acne client away since.

That's the shift the course is built to produce. Not better products. Better seeing.
