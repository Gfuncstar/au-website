Is £399 a lot for a skin programme?

Hi {first_name},

A question that comes up: is £399 a lot to spend on a skin programme?

Two ways to answer that.

The first is a comparison. An equivalent multi-condition skin specialist pathway with a private dermatology educator runs £600 to £900 per teaching day. The Skin Specialist Programme is ten modules, lifetime access, NICE-aligned, including the consultation framework and six conditions. Then add that one additional retained skin client at a confident £200 a treatment recoups the programme inside two visits.

The second is more honest. Most practitioners are confident in one or two skin conditions and quietly improvise on the rest. That's not negligence. It's the way the industry trains people. The problem is that improvising compounds: the clients you can't fully help drift away, you stop accepting consults you'd actually be able to handle if the framework were there, and the practice quietly narrows.

The Skin Specialist Programme is the one piece of CPD I built specifically to widen what a practitioner can confidently treat, without diluting the rigour. It is the curriculum I distilled from teaching inside the NHS, translated for private clinic practice. You don't take it to add another tick to a portfolio. You take it because clients want to see one practitioner for everything that's happening to their skin, and you'd rather be that practitioner.
