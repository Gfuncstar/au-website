# E6 — Practitioner outcome story (Day 11)

**Subject:** `What changed when she started consulting like a specialist`

**Preheader:** `One practitioner's quiet shift, after the Skin Specialist Programme.`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

A practitioner I taught last year told me something that stuck.

Before the programme, she described herself as "good at acne, okay
on rosacea, guessing on pigmentation." Her confidence sat with one
condition and dropped on either side of it. Half the consultation
enquiries that landed in her inbox were for things she felt
underprepared for, and she'd gently signpost them on to someone
else.

After the programme, she wrote to me and said the most useful
thing hadn't been any single condition module. It had been the
consultation framework. The structured intake, the history-taking,
the photography and pattern classification. Once that was in
place, every condition module slotted into the same shape, and she
stopped feeling like she had six different ways to think about
skin and started feeling like she had one way to think about skin
that worked across six conditions.

The shift wasn't that she suddenly knew everything. It was that
she knew which question to ask first, and the rest of the plan
followed.

Six months in, her diary looks different. The clients who used to
get signposted out now get retained. Her average treatment value
has climbed without her changing her prices, because the
documentation now justifies what the work is actually worth.

That's the shift the programme is built to produce. Not more
treatments. Better consulting.
