# E3 — The consultation framework (Day 5)

**Subject:** `The consultation that decides whether the plan holds`

**Preheader:** `Lessons 3 and 4 are ready. The structured framework underneath every defensible skin plan.`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

A confession. For my first few years working with skin, I treated
the consultation as the bit before the real work. The real work
was the treatment, the actives, the device, the protocol. The
consultation was a tick-box exercise, half-clinical and half-
chatty, that I rushed through to get to what I thought mattered.

I was wrong.

The consultation is the work. Everything else is delivery. If the
consultation is structured, the plan holds; if it isn't, the plan
unravels regardless of how technically good the treatment is. The
practitioners I see making the leap from confident-in-one-condition
to confident-across-conditions almost all describe it the same way:
they didn't change their hands, they changed their consultation.

Lesson 3 walks through the structured Skin Specialist consultation,
the same flow used inside the full programme: pre-consultation
intake, structured history, examination and photography, lesion
and pattern classification, expectations, consent. Lesson 4 is
already waiting after it, with the bridge to where this leads
next.
