Most skin plans don't fail because the practitioner is careless. They fail because the consultation skipped the question that would have changed the plan. The protocol is fine in isolation. The products are reasonable. The problem is that the protocol was written for the visible signs, not for the driver underneath them, and by week six the driver has the upper hand.

This is the gap that separates a skin therapist from a skin specialist. A therapist treats what's on the face. A specialist treats what's behind it, and the difference is almost always audible inside the first ten minutes of the consultation.

Lesson 2 walks through the most common version of this gap. What the consultation is doing instead of what it should be doing, and why "what are you using on your skin" is the question that costs practitioners more clients than any other.
