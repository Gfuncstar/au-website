A question that comes up: is £499 a lot to spend on a regulatory course?

Two ways to answer that.

The first is the numbers. One ASA complaint upheld, one Special Treatments Licence application refused on first submission, one adverse event without a written complication management plan in place. Any one of those events costs more than the Pathway, in fees, in lost weeks, and in the conversations you didn't expect to be having. Practitioners don't realise how much hidden insurance governance is, until the day they need it.

The second is more honest. Regulation is the part of the work nobody trains you on properly. Injection technique, yes. Skin science, yes. But the JCCP register, the CPSA framework, the ASA rulings, the incoming national licensing scheme, the way indemnity actually responds when a complaint lands, none of that shows up in foundation training, and most practitioners learn it piecemeal, by reading regulator updates between consultations and hoping the gaps don't matter.

The RAG Pathway is what stops it being piecemeal. It is the curriculum I distilled from running clinical workforce governance for an NHS Trust with oversight of more than a thousand nurses, translated for private aesthetic practitioners. Four weeks of structured work and you come out the other side with a clinic that's defensible end to end.

You don't need to memorise the regulations. You need a framework the regulator already recognises, in your own clinic, before they arrive looking for it.
