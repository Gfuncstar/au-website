# E2 — The Traffic Light System (Day 2)

**Subject:** `Red, Amber, Green: a way to think about every decision`

**Preheader:** `Day 2 is ready. The decision-making muscle that triages every procedure, claim and offer.`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

If you read Day 1, you'll already have a feel for where this goes.

Most practitioners don't get into trouble for being careless. They
get into trouble because the rules were never clearly explained to
them, and the regulators only spell things out once a problem has
already landed. So the practitioner improvises, follows the
visible practice they see online, and learns where the line was
when somebody else's clinic crosses it.

The Traffic Light System changes that. Red, Amber, Green. A
practical way to triage every new procedure, marketing claim and
treatment offer at a glance, before you commit to it. Red is "do
not, the regulator will." Amber is "you can, but only with the
documentation behind it." Green is "you're inside scope, here's
what to keep on file."

It's not a set of rules to memorise. It's a working muscle. Once
it's in place, every new offer that lands on your desk runs
through the same three-second triage in your head, and the
decisions get faster and more defensible at the same time.

Day 2 walks through the system end to end.
