By now you've worked through both days of the taster. The framework, the Traffic Light System, the difference between training and authorisation. Most practitioners I speak to say the same thing once they're through it: "this isn't as scary as I thought, but I now realise how much I didn't know."

Here's the reframing the taster doesn't have time for.

Compliance isn't paperwork to dread. Compliance is the foundation underneath your reputation. Two clinics in the same town, both visibly busy, both posting to Instagram, both selling treatments that look identical from the outside. One has the documentation trail to defend every decision. One doesn't. The first one's reputation compounds quietly across years. The second one's reputation collapses the first time something goes wrong, which it inevitably will, because clinic life runs long enough for something to land in everyone's inbox eventually.

Compliance gets you open. Reputation keeps you in business. The distance between the two is governance, the systems and the documentation that make every decision defensible after the fact.

The taster gives you the framework. What it can't give you in two short days is the document set, the audit checklists, the ASA-safe content modules, the consent and complaint workflows that turn the framework into the way your clinic actually runs.

That's the bridge the full RAG Pathway is built to cross. I'll write again in a couple of days about it. In the meantime, if you want to revisit any part of the taster, it's in the members area and yours to keep.
