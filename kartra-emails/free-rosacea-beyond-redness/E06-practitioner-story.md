# E6 — Practitioner outcome story (Day 10)

**Subject:** `What changed when she stopped chasing the redness`

**Preheader:** `One practitioner's quiet shift, after Rosacea Beyond Redness.`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

A practitioner I taught last year told me something that stuck.

Before the course, she was treating every rosacea client the same
way. Anti-redness serum, a soothing mask, a gentle peel, repeat.
About half her rosacea clients improved in the short term. The
other half came back warmer than they started, and she'd quietly
stopped taking new rosacea consults because she didn't trust her
own plan.

After the course, she wrote to me and said the most useful thing
hadn't been the topical ladder. It had been the subtype assessment
and the trigger-mapping protocol. She'd stopped opening with "what
products are you using" and started opening with "tell me when your
skin behaves and when it doesn't." Heat, alcohol, sun, stress,
gut, sleep. Then she'd map that against which subtype she was
seeing and build the plan around the driver, not the symptom.

The first time she did it for a client who'd seen three other
practitioners, she said the consultation took forty minutes and
the client cried at the end. Not because of the protocol. Because
somebody had finally read the condition the way she'd been
experiencing it.

She hasn't turned a rosacea client away since.

That's the shift the course is built to produce. Not better
products. Better seeing.
