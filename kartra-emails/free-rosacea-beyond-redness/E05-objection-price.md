# E5 — Price objection (Day 8)

**Subject:** `Is £150 worth it for one condition?`

**Preheader:** `The honest answer, with the maths laid out.`

---

## Body

Hi {first_name},

A question that comes up: is £150 a lot to spend on one condition?

Two ways to answer that.

The first is to count clients. If your client base sees rosacea
even occasionally, the course pays for itself the first time you
keep a client out of the flare-and-relapse cycle and on a barrier-
first plan that actually holds. One client retained, instead of
quietly drifting after week six when the anti-redness regimen stops
performing, and the course has earned its keep.

The second is more honest. Rosacea is the condition where outcomes
are slow, triggers are personal, and clients arrive having already
tried three regimens that didn't last. Most practitioners learn to
work around it. The ones who learn to manage rosacea properly end
up with a quiet stream of referrals, because the people who've been
let down by the last plan tell each other when they finally find
someone who reads the condition correctly.

You don't need a thousand rosacea clients. You need to be the
practitioner in your area whose name comes up when the last
anti-redness regimen didn't hold.
