A question that comes up: is £79 a lot to spend on one condition?

Two ways to answer that.

The first is to count clients. If your client base sees rosacea even occasionally, the course pays for itself the first time you keep a client out of the flare-and-relapse cycle and on a barrier- first plan that actually holds. One client retained, instead of quietly drifting after week six when the anti-redness regimen stops performing, and the course has earned its keep.

The second is more honest. Rosacea is the condition where outcomes are slow, triggers are personal, and clients arrive having already tried three regimens that didn't last. Most practitioners learn to work around it. The ones who learn to manage rosacea properly end up with a quiet stream of referrals, because the people who've been let down by the last plan tell each other when they finally find someone who reads the condition correctly.

You don't need a thousand rosacea clients. You need to be the practitioner in your area whose name comes up when the last anti-redness regimen didn't hold.
